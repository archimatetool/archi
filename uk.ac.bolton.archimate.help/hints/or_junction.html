A junction is used to connect dynamic relationships of the same type. A junction is used in a number of situations to connect dynamic (triggering or flow) relationships of the same type; e.g., to indicate splits or joins.

There are three types - junction, and and or (choice).

Category:

Other.

Examples:

An "assess application" business process can lead to a choice junction (split) to accept or reject the client application and route to alternative processes for each case.
